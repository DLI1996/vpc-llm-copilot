Do not accept VPC peering connections from unknown AWS accounts. A malicious user may have sent you a VPC peering connection request to gain unauthorized network access to your VPC. This is known as peer phishing. You can safely reject unwanted VPC peering connection requests without any risk of the requester gaining access to any information about your AWS account or your VPC. For more information, see Reject a VPC peering connection. You can also ignore the request and let it expire; by default, requests expire after 7 days.After you accept the VPC peering connection, you must add an entry to your route tables to enable traffic between the peered VPCs. For more information, see Update your route tables for a VPC peering connection.To accept a VPC peering connectionOpen the Amazon VPC console at https://console.aws.amazon.com/vpc/.Use the Region selector to choose the Region of the accepter VPC.
